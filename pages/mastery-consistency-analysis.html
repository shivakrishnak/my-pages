Root cause diagnosis

Problem 1

The system is a reference doc, not a daily driver

Your doc has 27+ skill sections across 5 parts. It's brilliant as a reference — but it's not designed to tell you "do exactly THIS for 20 minutes today." When you open it, the cognitive load of deciding what to do kills your momentum before you start.

Problem 2

65–80 min/day is too heavy for a new habit

Week 1 prescribes 65 minutes of structured practice on top of your work day. Research on habit formation (BJ Fogg, James Clear) shows this is 3–4x the threshold for sustainable new behaviors. You're not undisciplined — you set the bar too high from day 1.

Problem 3

No feedback loop — you can't see progress

The system measures skills over months (Section 48 says "results in weeks 4–8"). But your brain needs a dopamine signal every single day. Without a visible streak or checklist, your brain registers the effort as invisible — and invisible effort doesn't sustain.

Problem 4

Missing one day breaks the identity

You're tracking by streaks mentally. One missed day feels like the whole system collapsed. The Two-Day Rule (Section 26.5) is actually in your doc — but you haven't built it into how you think about the practice. Missing one day is data, not failure.

Root insight

The doc is the destination, not the map

You have a world-class system. What you're missing is a tiny, daily, non-negotiable front door to it — a 15-minute Minimum Viable Day that you can always do, no matter what. Section 40 of your own doc says this. The MVD is the answer.

"Never miss twice. The first miss is an accident. The second miss is the start of a new habit." — James Clear (your doc, Section 26.5)

90-day phased re-entry plan

Week 1–2

Identity first — 15 min only

Do ONLY the MVD (see tab 3). No more. Prove to yourself you can show up daily. Don't touch the full routine yet. The goal is: 14 days in a row.

Week 3–4

Add one habit stack

Month 2

Add 10 min Anki reviews daily. Each week, pick ONE section from Part II to focus on for the week (not all of them). Rotate through Sections 3, 5, 6, 9. Total: ~35 min.

Month 3

Full Phase 1 routine — now it feels earned

By now the habit groove is carved. The 65-min routine from your doc is your new normal because you built up to it. Add the Sunday Weekly Review (Section 38).

Your 3 non-negotiable rules

Rule 1 — Shrink the target, not your ambition

On hard days, do 5 minutes. Five minutes of vocal warm-up or shadowing counts. Showing up counts. You're building an identity: "I am someone who does this daily."

Rule 2 — Never miss twice

Miss one day? Fine. That's life. Miss two in a row? Emergency restart. One miss is allowed by design. Two misses is a pattern forming. React immediately.

Rule 3 — Pick ONE skill per week from the doc

Monday: choose one section. Practice only that this week. The rest of the doc is context — not today's job. This ends the overwhelm of "I should be doing everything."

What to track (visible progress)

Day streak (target: 14 → 30 → 60 → 90)

Track these monthly: (1) Record yourself speaking for 2 min — compare month to month. (2) Count filler words per minute — should drop. (3) One Anki deck size — should grow. These are your early signals, visible within weeks, not months.
