§28Daily Drill Library

A categorised reference of every drill in this system. Use this as a lookup — pick drills matching your current week's focus from §32–34.

28.1 Voice Drills (§3)

  • Diaphragmatic breathing — 4-in, hold 4, 8-out (2 min)
  • Straw phonation — hum through narrow straw (2 min)
  • Lip trills — vibrate lips sliding through pitch range (1 min)
  • Resonance shift — "mmmm-ahhh" moving vibration to face (2 min)
  • Wall check posture — speak with full-body wall contact (2 min)

    • 28.2 Pronunciation Drills (§4)

  • Minimal pairs — 10 reps per pair, V/W, TH, æ/ɛ (5 min)
  • Rubber band stress drill — physical stretch on stressed syllable (5 min)
  • Mirror + slow motion — Youglish.com word study (10 min)
  • Tongue twisters — slow → fast, 2–3 rotating weekly (5 min)
  • Shadowing — Level 1/2/3 progression (10 min)

    • 28.3 Fluency Drills (§5)

  • 2-minute monologue — never stop talking (2 min)
  • 4-3-2 technique — same topic, decreasing time (9 min)
  • Record & count fillers — weekly tracking (5 min)
  • Formulaic chunks — say 5 phrases aloud 5× each (5 min)
  • Thinking-in-English narration — all day, ambient practice

    • 28.4 Structure & Thinking Drills (§6, §9–11)

  • PREP response — live in a meeting (ambient)
  • Pyramid rewrite — take an email, rewrite conclusion-first (10 min)
  • Teach the wall — explain something aloud, no notes (5 min)
  • Five Whys — root-cause depth drill (5 min)
  • Mental Model Journal — apply one model to a real event (10 min/week)
  • Retrieval rehearsal — explain a learned concept from memory (5 min)

    • 28.5 Public Speaking Drills (§7)

  • Pre-speech ritual — power pose, sigh, warm-up, intention (5 min)
  • Pause drill — 2-sec pause before key points in monologue (5 min)
  • Stage presence — mirror practice of 4 pillars (10 min)
  • Hot seat — rapid-fire Q&A with framework responses (10 min/week)

    • §29Weekly Challenges

    One stretch challenge per week — beyond the daily routine — that forces real-world application under social stakes.

    Week Challenge
    1 Record your voice once and notice your natural pitch and pace
    2 Use one new vocabulary word correctly in a real conversation
    3 Go a full day narrating your actions in English internally
    4 Explain a technical concept to a non-technical friend or family member
    5 Use PREP explicitly in a real meeting at least twice
    6 Apply one mental model consciously to a real decision this week
    7 Rewrite every email you send this week in BLUF format
    8 Use a bridge phrase deliberately when you feel brain freeze coming
    9 Volunteer to present or speak in a meeting you'd normally stay silent in
    10 Complete one full week with a protected 90-min deep work block daily
    11 Practice Level 3 listening in every 1-on-1 this week
    12 Deliver your "month 3 recording" topic live to a real person, not just record it

    §30Conversation Simulations

    Simulating high-pressure conversations in low-pressure settings builds the retrieval pathways (§1.5) that real situations require. Practice these scenarios solo (recorded) or with a partner.

    30.1 The Difficult Feedback Simulation

    Scenario: You need to tell a teammate their code review comments have been too harsh. Practice using SBI (§12.3) — situation, behaviour, impact — out loud, twice, refining each time.

    30.2 The Unprepared Question Simulation

    Have someone ask you 5 unrelated questions back to back — work-related and personal. Answer each in under 60 seconds using PREP or a bridge phrase. This is the single best drill for §16 brain freeze elimination.

    30.3 The Pushback Simulation

    Have a partner disagree with your technical proposal aggressively. Practice staying calm, using tactical empathy (§15.5), and restating your position using PREP without becoming defensive.

    30.4 The Salary Negotiation Simulation

    Role-play a negotiation. Practice not naming a number first, anchoring high when you do, and sitting in silence after stating your ask (§12.7).

    30.5 The Executive Update Simulation

    Give a 60-second project update as if to a VP who has zero context and 60 seconds of attention. Practice Pyramid Principle — conclusion first, 2–3 reasons, no unnecessary detail.

    §31Recording & Self-Review Protocol

    Recording yourself is uncomfortable and one of the highest-ROI practices in this entire system. You cannot accurately self-assess your speaking without hearing it back — your internal sense of how you sound is unreliable.

    31.1 What to Record

    • Daily 2-minute monologues (keep only weekly samples, not every day)
    • Monthly baseline comparison (same topic — Day 1, 30, 60, 90)
    • Mock presentations or important real meetings (with permission)
    • STAR stories and prepared answers

    31.2 The Self-Review Checklist

  • Filler word count (um, like, basically, only thing is)
  • Pace — too fast, too slow, or varied appropriately?
  • Pauses — present and used deliberately, or absent?
  • Vocal variety — monotone or expressive pitch/volume/rate?
  • Structure — was there a clear point? Could you identify PREP or Pyramid?
  • Filler phrases — "I think", "kind of", "sort of" overused?
  • One thing that improved since last recording
  • One thing to focus on next

    • 31.3 The Compare-Don't-Judge Principle

    Compare this recording only to your own past recordings — never to a polished public speaker. You're tracking your own trajectory, not benchmarking against TED speakers with decades of practice. Progress over 90 days is the only meaningful comparison.

    §32Phase 1 — Foundation (Days 1–30)

    Goal: build the daily habit. 15→25 minutes/day. Sections covered: §3 Voice, §4 Pronunciation, §5 Fluency, §9C Articulation. The only success metric in Phase 1 is consistency — not quality.

    Phase 1 mantra: Show up daily, even imperfectly. A reduced 8-minute session counts as a full day. Never miss twice (§26.5).

    For the complete day-by-day breakdown of Phase 1 with specific daily exercises, see the 90-Day Action Plan — Month 1 tab.

    §33Phase 2 — Structure (Days 31–60)

    Goal: add thinking frameworks and apply them at work. 25→40 minutes/day. Sections covered: §6 Structured Speaking, §11 Mental Models, §12 Workplace Communication, §16 Brain Freeze.

    Phase 2 mantra: Every framework must hit a real situation within 48 hours of learning it. Application converts learning into skill.

    For the complete day-by-day breakdown of Phase 2, see the 90-Day Action Plan — Month 2 tab.

    §34Phase 3 — Integration & Acceleration (Days 61–90)

    Goal: integrate everything simultaneously; build identity, not just skill. 40→50 minutes/day. Sections covered: §7 Public Speaking, §9A Deep Understanding, §14 Conversation Intelligence, §20 Deep Work, §21 Stoicism.

    Phase 3 mantra: You are no longer building skills in isolation — you are becoming the person who has them. Identity, not technique, is the final outcome.

    For the complete day-by-day breakdown of Phase 3, and for planning Month 4 onward, see the 90-Day Action Plan — Month 3 tab.

    2. Anki review (~20 cards) — 8 min

    4. Shadowing L2 — 8 min

    2. Anki review (~35 cards) — 10 min

  • Tue–Fri — Daily routine + one specific drill tied to this week's section
  • Saturday — Teach the wall (extended, 5–10 min) + Anki review of all cards

    §38The Sunday Review Protocol

    Sunday Review — 20 minutes
    1. How many days did I practice this week? (out of 7)
    2. What felt easy? What felt hard?
    3. Which section surprised me or changed how I think?
    4. Did I apply anything at work this week? What happened?
    5. What is my one non-negotiable focus for next week?
    6. Am I overwhelmed? If yes — what can I remove, not add?

    §39The Recording Protocol

    1. Choose a fixed topic for all four recordings (Day 1, 30, 60, 90) — e.g., "What I do at work and where I want to be in 2 years"
    2. Same location, same setup each time, if possible — controls for environment variables
    3. Speak for exactly 2 minutes, unscripted
    4. Save with clear filenames: baseline_day1, recording_day30, recording_day60, recording_day90
    5. At Day 90, listen to all four back-to-back. This comparison is your hard evidence of growth.

    §40The Minimum Viable Day (MVD)

    The single most important concept for long-term consistency. On any day where the full routine feels impossible — exhaustion, travel, crisis — do only this:

    MVD — 8 minutes, non-negotiable
    1. Vocal warm-up — straw phonation + lip trills (3 min)
    2. Read one paragraph aloud (2 min)
    3. Teach the wall — one thing you know, 60 seconds (3 min)
    This counts as a full day. The goal of the MVD is not skill development — it's identity preservation. You remain "someone who practises daily" even on your worst day. That identity is worth more than any single session's content.
