§45Anki Deck Starters — Copy These Cards

Front / Back format. Copy these directly into Anki to start your Tier 1 deck.

FrontBack
What is PREP?Point → Reason → Example → Point. Best for meetings, Q&A, standups.
What is the Pyramid Principle?Conclusion first, then 2–3 supporting arguments, then data. For email/written comms.
What is First Principles thinking?Break a problem to fundamental truths; reason upward instead of by analogy.
What is Inversion?Ask how to guarantee failure, then avoid those things.
What is Second-Order Thinking?Ask what happens next — and then what happens after that.
What is the Two-Day Rule?Never miss a habit twice in a row. One miss is an accident; two is a new habit forming.
What is the Feynman Technique?Explain a concept simply as if to a 12-year-old; gaps in your explanation reveal gaps in understanding.
What is RASA?Receive, Appreciate, Summarize, Ask — active listening framework.
What is SBI feedback?Situation → Behaviour → Impact. Specific, non-judgmental feedback structure.
What is the dichotomy of control?Stoic principle: separate what's in your control (thoughts, actions) from what isn't (outcomes, others' opinions). Focus only on the former.

§4712 Principles to Pin on Your Wall

01
Systems beat motivation
Build the system, protect the system, let the system build you.
02
Never miss twice
One miss is an accident. Two is a new habit forming.
03
Retrieval beats re-reading
Closing the book and recalling is worth more than ten re-reads.
04
If you can't explain it simply…
…you don't understand it well enough. — Feynman
05
Conclusion first, always
Pyramid Principle. Busy people want the answer before the reasoning.
06
The pause is a tool, not a failure
Silence sounds confident. "Um" sounds uncertain.
07
Invert the problem
How would I guarantee failure? Now avoid those things.
08
Focus only on what's in your control
The dichotomy of control. Release the rest.
09
Apply within 48 hours
Knowledge becomes skill only through real application, fast.
10
Identity, not outcome
"I am someone who practises daily" beats "I want to get better."
11
Compare only to your past self
Progress is measured against Day 1, never against anyone else.
12
You will not feel it day by day
Then one day, you will notice you already are.

§48Expected Timeline of Results

Week 1–2 — No visible change yet. This is normal. You're building retrieval pathways that don't show external results immediately.
Week 3–4 — You start noticing your own fillers more (awareness precedes elimination). Voice warm-up feels automatic.
Week 5–8 — Frameworks (PREP, Pyramid) start appearing naturally in real meetings without conscious effort. First visible changes in recordings.
Week 9–12 — Brain freeze frequency drops noticeably. Colleagues may comment on clearer communication. Identity shift solidifies — you start to think of yourself differently.
Month 4–6 — Genuinely different communicator. Frameworks are unconscious competence. Mental models actively shape your decision-making, not just your speaking.
The honest caveat: This timeline assumes consistent practice at 80%+ adherence. Results compound — Month 6 is not 2× Month 3, it's closer to 5–6× because retrieval pathways strengthen non-linearly with repetition.

§49How to Read This Document

This system has 49 sections across 5 parts and is intentionally comprehensive — built as a permanent reference, not a single read-through. Here is how to actually use it without being overwhelmed:

  1. Read Part I once — understand the science. This explains why everything else works.
  2. Don't read Part II linearly — it's a reference. Pull one section at a time, matched to your current week in the 90-day plan.
  3. Use Part III as a drill lookup — when you need a specific exercise, find it here.
  4. Follow Part IV as your operating system — the day-by-day, week-by-week structure that tells you exactly what to do today.
  5. Return to Part V when you need a resource — a book, a tool, an Anki card, or a reminder of the core principles.
The golden rule: One doc section per week. The rest of the document is context, not today's job. Close the doc when you're done reading your section — open it again next week.
