Your 90-Day Action Plan

Day-by-day, week-by-week. Tap through tabs below or use the sidebar. Every week links directly to its matching section in the main system.

Your 90-Day Mastery Mission

Three months. One daily routine that grows week by week. One doc section per week. By day 90 the practice will be who you are — not something you need motivation for.

The core commitment
15 minutes a day in Month 1 → 30 minutes in Month 2 → 50 minutes in Month 3. Built one week at a time. Never overwhelming because you never jump ahead.

Three phases

Month 1 · Days 1–30
Foundation — Build the groove
15→25 min/day. Voice + fluency + 5 Anki cards. The only goal: show up every single day. Doc sections: §3 Voice, §4 Pronunciation, §5 Fluency, §9C Articulation.
Month 2 · Days 31–60
Structure — Add frameworks and thinking
25→40 min/day. PREP, Pyramid Principle, 10 mental models, workplace communication, brain freeze drills. Apply at work daily. Doc sections: §6, §11, §12, §16.
Month 3 · Days 61–90
Integration — Become the person
40→50 min/day. Public speaking, deep work, stoicism, conversation intelligence. Weekly recordings. You're building identity now, not just skills. Doc sections: §7, §9A, §14, §20, §21.

What you'll feel by Day 90

→
Your standups and meeting contributions will be crisper and more confident
→
Brain freeze will drop significantly — you'll have bridge phrases on reflex
→
You'll catch yourself thinking in frameworks (PREP, Pyramid) automatically
→
Written communication — Slack, email — will feel cleaner and more direct
→
You'll have proof to yourself that you are someone who follows through

Voice Development

Doc section this week: §3 Voice Development. Goal: build the daily warm-up habit. 15 min/day. Nothing else yet.

§3 Voice Development →
This week's single focus
Your voice is the instrument. Before you can work on what you say, you need to know how your instrument works. This week is purely physical — breathing, resonance, posture. Boring? Yes. Foundational? Absolutely.

Day-by-day plan

Mon
Vocal warm-up (§3.3) + Read aloud 1 paragraph
Focus: diaphragmatic breathing. Lie on the floor with a book on your stomach. Watch it rise and fall as you breathe.
10 min
Tue
Vocal warm-up + Straw phonation drill (§3.1B)
2 min straw. Hum through a coffee straw. Feel vibration move from throat to face. This warms up your vocal cords safely.
10 min
Wed
Vocal warm-up + Resonance shift drill (§3.1C)
"mmmm-ahhh" 10 times. Feel it in your nose bridge. Then chest. Then face. Resonance = carrying power without volume.
10 min
Thu
Vocal warm-up + Wall check posture (§3.5) + read aloud
Heels, butt, shoulders, back of head all touching the wall. Speak for 2 minutes in that posture. Feel the difference.
12 min
Fri
Vocal warm-up + 2-min monologue on any topic
Do not stop. If stuck, describe the room, then return to your topic. The rule: mouth never stops moving for 2 minutes.
12 min
Sat
Anki review (5 cards) + Teach the wall (§9C.5)
Pick one mental model from your 5 Anki cards. Explain it aloud for 60 seconds to the wall. No notes.
15 min
Sun
Rest or catch-up day + Mini weekly review
Ask yourself: Did I do 5+ of 6 days? What felt hard? What felt easy? Write 3 lines in a notebook. No judgment.
10 min

§4 Pronunciation Training →
This week's single focus
Pronunciation is mechanical — it's mouth position and muscle memory. V/W confusion, TH sounds, and stress patterns are the three biggest shifts for Indian English speakers. You drill them like a musician practises scales.

Day-by-day plan

Daily

5 steps, 5 minutes. This never changes for the next 90 days.
5 min
Mon
Read §4.1 Mirror technique + §4.2 Minimal pairs drill
V/W pairs in mirror: vine/wine, vet/wet, very/wary. 10x each. Watch your bottom lip touch your top teeth for V.
10 min
Tue
TH sounds drill (§4.2) + Tongue twisters (§4.7)
"Thirty-three thieves thought they thrilled the throne." Slow first, then speed up. Tongue goes between teeth for TH.
10 min
Wed
Word stress — rubber band exercise (§4.3)
comPUter, photOGraphy, underSTAND, preSENtation. Stretch a rubber band on the stressed syllable. Feel it physically.
10 min

Pick a 2-min TED clip (Simon Sinek "Start with Why" opening is ideal). Shadow with transcript in front of you.
10 min
Fri
Same TED clip — shadow again + 2-min free monologue
Same clip, same 2 minutes. Repetition is the point. Then a 2-min monologue on your week.
13 min
Sat
Anki review + add 3 new cards
New cards: TH sound rule · V vs W mouth position · Primary word stress rule (content words stressed, function words unstressed).
15 min

The 10 Tier 1 mental models to know this week

1
First Principles — break things down to fundamental truths, reason up from there
2
Inversion — ask how to fail, then avoid those things
3
Second-Order Thinking — what happens next, and then what happens after that?
4
Incentives — people respond to incentives; understand what someone wants to understand their behaviour
5
Opportunity Cost — choosing one thing means not choosing everything else
6
Occam's Razor — the simplest explanation is usually correct
7
Hanlon's Razor — never attribute to malice what can be explained by incompetence or miscommunication
8
Map vs Territory — the model is not the reality; all models are wrong, some are useful
9
Circle of Competence — know what you know, know what you don't
10
Probabilistic Thinking — think in probabilities, not certainties

The Rules

Pin these somewhere you see them daily. These override everything else.

Rule 1 — Never miss twice
One miss is an accident. Two misses in a row is the start of a new (bad) habit. The moment you miss a day, the next day becomes the most important day of the 90. React immediately.
Rule 2 — On hard days, do only the first 3 items
Warm-up + read aloud + Teach the Wall. Eight minutes. That counts as a full day. Showing up at reduced capacity is infinitely better than not showing up. The habit is more important than the session quality in Month 1.
Rule 3 — One doc section per week, nothing more
The doc has 27+ skill sections. You will read one per week. The rest of the doc is context — not today's job. This is what ends the overwhelm. One section. Drill it all week. Move on.
Rule 4 — Apply at work, not just in practice
Every framework must hit a real situation within 48 hours of learning it. PREP in a standup. Pyramid in an email. Mental model in a decision. Application is what converts learning into skill.
Rule 5 — Don't add Anki cards for everything
Only add a card if you will use that information in the next 30 days AND forgetting it would cost you. Aim for 3–5 new cards per week max. A small, consistent deck beats a large, abandoned one.
Rule 6 — The decision is already made
Stop asking yourself each morning "should I do this today?" The answer is yes. You decided that on Day 1. You are someone who practises every day. This is not a daily choice — it's an identity.

The two-day rule (§26.5 of your doc)

"Never miss twice. The first miss is an accident. The second miss is the start of a new habit." — James Clear, Atomic Habits. This single rule, consistently applied, is worth more than any technique in the system.

What to do when you feel overwhelmed

1
Close the doc. You don't need it right now.
The doc is a reference. Today's job is the daily routine, not reading more sections.
2
Do only the 8-minute MVD.
Warm-up + read aloud + Teach the Wall. That's today done.
3
Ask: what is this week's one section?
Only one. Drill it. Everything else can wait.
4
Remember: you are not learning everything. You are building one person.
The goal is not to know the whole doc. The goal is to become someone who communicates and thinks exceptionally well.
"Systems beat motivation. Build the system, protect the system, and let the system build you." — Your doc, §26.1
