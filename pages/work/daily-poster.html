Roadblock Cue Cards — What to do when...
😴 Feeling sleepy while reading
1
Stop reading immediately
Reading while drowsy has near-zero retention. You're wasting time, not studying.
2
20-min nap (set alarm for 25 min)
NASA study: 26-min nap = 26% performance boost. 1–3 PM only. Don't fight it — use it.
3
Or: switch to active mode instead
Stop reading. Write what you know from memory. Draw the concept. Talk out loud. Movement beats drowsiness.
4
Cold water on face + 10 squats
Triggers norepinephrine release. Works in 60 seconds. Better than coffee at 3 PM.
5
Check: did you eat a heavy lunch?
Post-lunch fog = blood diverted to digestion. Eat lighter. Curd + rice + sabzi, not biryani.

Root cause of daily sleepiness during study = previous night's poor sleep. Fix the night, fix the day.

🧱 Stuck on a concept — nothing is clicking
1
Write what you DO know first
Start with what's clear. Often the gap becomes obvious once you write the boundary.
2
Go one level simpler
If ConcurrentHashMap isn't clicking, go back to HashMap. Always find the floor you're standing on.
3
Change the medium
Stop reading. Draw it. Talk to yourself. Find a YouTube video just to SEE it. Understanding unlocks at different angles.
4
Write your confusion specifically
"I understand X but I don't understand why Y." Precision in the question accelerates finding the answer.
5
Walk away for 20 minutes
Insight often arrives during rest. Default mode network processes what focused attention can't. Walk. Come back.
😑 Can't start — everything feels heavy
1
5 — 4 — 3 — 2 — 1 — GO
Count backwards and physically open the laptop. The resistance lives before the start, not after it.
2
2-minute rule: just 2 minutes
Tell yourself: "I'll study for just 2 minutes." You'll almost never stop at 2 minutes. Starting is the whole game.
3
Do the easiest thing first
Review yesterday's flashcards. Don't start with new hard material. Build momentum with something you already know.
4
Name what's actually wrong
Is it fear? Overwhelm? Boredom? Tiredness? Name it. Write it. Then ask: "What's the one smallest next action?"
5
Today's MVD — deploy it
One block. One job application. One walk. That's a win. Do that. Only that. Don't negotiate more.
📱 Doom-scrolled and wasted 2 hours
1
Put the phone in another room. Now.
Not face down. Not silent. Another room. This is the only fix. Willpower alone doesn't work against engineered feeds.
2
Don't catastrophise the wasted time
2 hours gone ≠ the day is over. 3 hours of quality study remain. That is still a productive day.
3
5-4-3-2-1 and start the next block
The only recovery is action. Not guilt. Not planning a better tomorrow. Action now.
4
Set app time limits tonight
Use ScreenTime (iPhone) to limit Instagram/YouTube to 20 min/day. Make it require a password someone else sets.

You are not weak for getting sucked in. These apps spent $100M engineering that pull. You need a structural barrier, not more willpower.

😰 Overwhelm / panic / "I'll never be ready"
1
Physiological sigh — right now
Double inhale (nose), long exhale (mouth). Twice. Resets the nervous system in under 10 seconds.
2
Name the specific fear
"I'm scared I don't know X." Not "I'll never be ready." Specific fear = solvable. Vague dread = spiral.
3
Read your evidence list
Open your journal. Find one thing you nailed this week. You have 12 years of nailing things. This is not your ceiling.
4
Zoom out: what is actually true?
You have paid runway. You have a plan. You have a study system. You showed up today. That is more than most candidates.
5
Do the next 25 minutes, nothing more
Don't think about the offer deadline. Don't think about 45 days. Think about the next Pomodoro only.
💔 Bad week — feel behind, off track
1
One missed day is noise. A week needs a reset.
Saturday morning reset: honest 20-min review. What happened? What's one thing to fix? Only one.
2
Shrink the plan until it's easy
If the full schedule isn't working, go MVD for 3 days. Rebuild momentum at minimum before expanding again.
3
Look at what you DID do
Write a "done list" not a "to-do list." What did you actually accomplish? Progress is always more than it feels.
4
Don't restart from scratch
Every time you feel behind and restart the whole system, you lose the compounding. Continue from exactly where you are.
5
Remember what's actually at stake
Paid leave ends. Savings run out. You have a real timeline and a real reason. That's not pressure — that's clarity.

The system is not the enemy. Your perfectionism is. A messy, inconsistent journey with the system beats a perfect plan that restarts every Monday.
