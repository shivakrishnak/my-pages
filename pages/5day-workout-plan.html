Mallapur Five

A 5-day Upper/Lower split built for general fitness, endurance, and strength — not bodybuilding. 45–60 min sessions, lower-back safe, evidence-based.

34M · 70kg · 5'6" · Some experience · Slight back pain · 5 days/week

Your Weekly Plan

Five compound-lift sessions a week, alternating upper and lower body, with one dedicated cardio day. Every session opens with a hip-hinge or core-stability move chosen specifically for your lower back sensitivity.

Why This Split
Upper/lower alternation gives each muscle group ~48 hours to recover while training twice a week — optimal for strength and endurance per ACSM/NSCA guidelines.
Why Few Exercises
5–6 compound movements per session builds more real-world fitness than 15 isolation exercises, and is far easier to recover from consistently.

Saturday & Sunday — Rest / Active Recovery

Rest days are when your muscles repair and grow stronger — they're not optional, they're part of the plan.

What To Do
A relaxed 30–40 min walk on one or both days. Light stretching — hips, hamstrings, lower back. Playing with your kids counts. Prioritise sleep — this is where most recovery happens.
What To Avoid
Heavy lifting or high-intensity training. Sitting completely still all day — gentle movement helps back pain more than total rest. Skipping both rest days even if you feel energetic.

Principles & Progression Rules

The rules that make this plan work over months, not just one week.

Progressive Overload
The 2-for-2 rule: If you complete all prescribed sets and reps with good form for 2 consecutive sessions on an exercise, add a small amount of weight next time — 2.5kg for upper body, 2.5–5kg for lower body. If you miss reps or form breaks down, stay at the same weight.
Starting Weights
First 1–2 weeks should feel like "could do a few more reps." This lets you learn each movement safely before adding real load. The numbers in this plan are starting points, not targets.
Tracking Progress
Keep a simple phone note: date, exercise, weight, reps done. 5 minutes a week of tracking turns "going to the gym" into measurable lasting gains.
Rest Between Sets
Compound lower-body lifts: 75–90 sec. Upper body compound: 60–90 sec. Isolation and core: 45 sec. Resting properly is part of the program, not wasted time.
Sleep & Nutrition
Aim for 7+ hours sleep and adequate protein (~1.2–1.6g per kg bodyweight daily = ~85–110g for you). No exercise program outperforms poor sleep or poor eating.
Back Pain — Your Specific Guidance
Your pattern: Slight back pain from carrying toddlers and long sitting responds very well to exactly this kind of training: hip hinge strength (RDL, hip thrust), anti-extension core work (planks, dead bugs, bird dogs), and glute strengthening. These rebuild the support structure around your spine.
Always Do
Brace your core before any loaded lift. Keep your lower back's natural curve during squats and hinges. Stop immediately if you feel sharp or shooting pain (vs. normal muscle fatigue).
Avoid For Now
Heavy barbell deadlifts from the floor (until hip-hinge pattern is confident). Loaded spinal flexion (weighted sit-ups, heavy Russian twists). Lifting kids by bending your back — bend knees and hips instead.
Not medical advice. Since you have recurring back pain, consider a quick check with a doctor or physio before starting — especially if pain is frequent, sharp, or radiates down a leg.

Mallapur Five — adjust weights to your comfort. Always prioritise form over load. Re-check progress every 4–6 weeks.
